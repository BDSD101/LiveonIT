How we score

Every address gets a score out of 10. The higher the score, the more of your daily life you can do on foot. Here's exactly what goes into it.

Closest Services

85%

This is the big one. We look at how far you'd have to walk to reach each of the services you've selected — your supermarket, your café, your gym, whatever matters to you — and we weight them by how often a typical person needs them.

A supermarket counts a lot more than a dentist, because you go to one every week and the other maybe twice a year. Services within a 5-minute walk score perfectly. Anything beyond 20 minutes scores zero. Everything in between gets a score on a sliding scale.

Errand Walk

5%

Picture leaving home, hitting your supermarket, grabbing a coffee, stopping by the park, then getting back — all in one loop without backtracking too much. We calculate the most efficient route between your everyday services and score it based on how long that walk would be. A tight cluster of services scores well; services scattered across a wide area score poorly.

Choice & Variety

5%

Having one supermarket 750m away is fine. Having three within 200m is better — if one's closed, or you just prefer another, you have options. This component counts how many of each service exist within walking distance and rewards suburbs where there's genuine competition and variety.

House Price

2.5%

Walkable suburbs tend to be more expensive. We factor in relative house prices so that a suburb scoring well on walkability but costing significantly more than average doesn't get a free ride. More affordable suburbs get a modest boost here.

Safety

2.5%

We use crime data at the suburb level to apply a small safety adjustment. This isn't a dominant factor — it's a tiebreaker at the margins rather than something that will swing your score dramatically either way.

If house price or safety data isn't available for a suburb, the remaining components are scaled up proportionally so the score always adds to 100%.

A few things worth knowing

Your score is personal

The score reflects the services you selected, not every possible service in the area. If you only care about a supermarket and a train station, your score is calculated on just those two things. Change your selections and the score updates to match.

What "walkable" means here

We define walkable using the Victorian Government's 20-Minute Neighbourhood standard — everything you need for daily life should be within a 20-minute return walk from your front door, or roughly 800 metres each way at a normal walking pace.

Read the Victorian Government policy

No scoring system is perfect

This tool is designed to help you compare addresses quickly and consistently — not replace the experience of walking the streets yourself. Use the score as a starting point, not the final word.

What is a 20-Minute Neighbourhood?

The 20-minute neighbourhood concept means residents can meet most of their everyday needs within a 20-minute return walk from home — roughly an 800-metre radius at a comfortable walking pace. This idea is at the heart of Victoria's long-term planning strategy, with goals around healthier communities, lower car dependence, and more sustainable cities.
