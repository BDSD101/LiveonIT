Scoring Methodology & Framework

An analytical approach to measuring neighbourhood walkability and service accessibility in Metropolitan Melbourne.

I. Overview

The 20-Minute Neighbourhood Explorer utilizes a weighted multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate the accessibility and liveability of any given address. Each location is assigned a cumulative score out of 10.0, representing the degree to which daily requirements can be met within a local return walk.

The framework is grounded in the Victorian Government's Plan Melbourne 2017–2050 strategy, prioritizing proximity, variety, and safety.

Service Frequency Categorization

Not all services carry equal weight within the "Closest Services" component. Our algorithm categorizes services by their typical usage frequency to ensure that essential daily needs influence the score more significantly than occasional requirements.

III. Operational Parameters

Subjective Service Selection

Unlike static walkability indices, this framework is dynamic. The score is calculated strictly based on the services selected by the user. If an individual only requires access to a library and a train station, the final rating will reflect only those specific accessibility needs, rather than a generic average of all possible amenities.

The "20-Minute" Standard

The definition of "walkable" is derived from the Victorian Government standard: a 20-minute return walk. This equates to an approximately 800-metre radius (10 minutes each way) at an average pedestrian pace. Services within 400 metres (5-minute walk) receive a full accessibility score, decaying to zero at the 800-metre threshold.

Data Limitations & Interpretability

While this model provides a consistent quantitative baseline for comparison, it is not an exhaustive measure of neighbourhood quality. Factors such as pavement quality, topographical changes, and shade cover are not currently accounted for in the primary scoring engine.

Strategic Policy Context

The 20-minute neighbourhood concept is a cornerstone of the Plan Melbourne 2017–2050 strategy. It aims to create vibrant, local communities where residents can meet most of their daily needs locally, fostering better health, stronger social connections, and reduced environmental impact.
